Engagement Story

Rich proposed to me in my kitchen on new years day while we were cooking dinner (a great start to the year. So far 2007 my all time favourite).

We had just come back from a glorious holiday in Jamaica. We had gone there so that we could spend Christmas there and I could meet his family. And it was a beautiful and fantastic country. We had great time there and loved spending so much time with each other and with his family. One of the best holidays.

And so we returned with the firm idea of getting married sometime in the new year. But the question of 'when' still remained. So I was sort of expecting a proposal but...

Anyway, we were in my kitchen on new year's day cooking dinner. I got up to go and get the peas out of the freezer and saw Rich come up behind me. When I turned around I saw that he had a very serious look on his face and so I hugged him and smiled. He then said, "Heather, will you marry me?". And I was initially so taken aback I didn't know what to think. Was this real? And it was! I was so shocked and thrilled and happy! Dropped the frozen peas (which I am now convinced are now a vital feature of any romance) and said, "yes, yes, yes". We then almost burnt supper and had to throw away most of it as no appetite (ahhh, love).

The next day (Tues) we went through to the diamond district in London (called Hatton Gardens) with the hope of maybe looking in some windows at something and getting an idea of what we wanted for engagement rings. We thought most places would be closed. But there was one shop open and the owner (a little orthodox Jewish man) ushered us in, showed us a whole range of rings and we found one we both loved. Easy :-) And then we went to a posh restaurant and I got another proposal with champagne. And then back to my place for coffee and my third proposal - this time on bended knee. And in case you're wondering, I said yes every time.

Most things so far in the planning have proved to be easier than expected and we are now in the happy position of having a date for the wedding (June 2007), the church and minister and a reception venue booked.

So onward to the new day. Will keep you posted with further developments.

14 January 2007

More venue shopping.

We saw two potential venues for our wedding reception today. The basic plan is to have an afternoon wedding, starting around 2pm, then the wedding party will have photos while dinner guests head from the church to the venue, where we will join them once the happy snappy bit is done. We will then proceed to eat drink and generally be merry. Then, in the evening there will be music and dancing, with more guests joining us for that phase of the evening. The guest lists are still evolving, we're just happy to have a clear idea of what we want, and it seems like we are making progress. I don't want to put up any details of the venue until it's all set, hopefully very soon.

About Us


In case you don't know us both, here's a tiny shapshot of who we are and how we met.

Richard is Jamaican, and has been living in London since July 2002. He's an enthusiastic sailor, not-so-enthusiastic long distance runner and an avid technophile. He works in the City as a technical consultant providing computer software programming services to large large investment banks.

Heather is South African and has been living at various places in London and South-East England since January 2002. She likes the outdoors, reading and baking. She is a Speech and Language Therapist working for the NHS.


We met 1st July 2006 on a sailing trip in Greece. After seven days of adventure including major storms on the first day of the trip we definitely were interested in each other and started dating when we got back to London.

We're engaged!!


On the 1st of January 2007 I proposed to Heather, and she said yes!! There's a funny story to the proposal, which I may put up here later. We went shopping for the engagement ring together in London's Hatton Gardens diamond district. That was actually really easy, we found a jeweller who was open late and he showered us with a variety of styles, before we found the right one, a yellow gold band with a white gold setting for a solitaire round-cut diamond.

We're planning the wedding for the 2nd of June 2007, at St Helen's church Bishopsgate, in London. That's the church we both attend and having the service means a lot to us as we start our new life together.
