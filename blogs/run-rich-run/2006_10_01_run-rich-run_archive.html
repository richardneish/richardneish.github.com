Wednesday, October 18, 2006

From work to Heather's flat

From 99 Bishopsgate, ran down towards the river, going through a few random alleyways and getting slightly lost. Ended up going past Cannon St Station, then popped out at the river near Southwark Bridge, where I was able to get down to the THames path. I ran into a dead end and had to double back at one point, but ended up on the Embankment eventually. Followed the river round to Westminster, then swung north, round the Houses of Parliment, and along the roadside to Chelsea Bridge.

Over the bridge, then Queenstown Road to the intersection with Lavender Hill, where I stopped at a Sainsbury's Local to pick up dinner, then headed on th Heather's flat on Jedburg Street. Distance to the first stop was just over 9 km in 58 minutes, total distance was just under 10 km.

Friday, October 13, 2006

Route home from work

On Wednesday, I ran home from Rule Financial, on New Broad Street. The route goes past Liverpool Street Station, along Great Eastern Street to Old Street. Then up the A1200 (New North Road / Canonbury Road) to Highbury corner. Then the long slog up Holloway road to Archway. The grand finale is up the Archway Road (great big hill) to my flat.

The route distance is just over 8km, and with the hill and a pack on my back it took just under 48 minutes. I think this will be a good workout to keep up. My shoulders and neck are a bit stiff, but no problems apart from that. The last time I did this route (or similar) I didn't have the pack, but it was raining. Overall I preferred the conditions this time around. :-)

Monday, October 09, 2006

Nike RunLondon 10K - New PB!

Ran the Nike RunLondon 10K yesterday in Hyde Park. This makes the third time I have done this annual event (previous runs were 2002 and 2005). The course was the same as last year, which made a difference, since I was able to remember where I got into trouble last time and keep a good mental image of the distance left to run. Conditions on race day were good, sunny, no wind, cool, but not as cold as last year. Got there with plenty of time in hand and could have been right at the front of the pen. Decided to step back a few rows to let the eager types charge off so I could run my own race.

This meant that I didn't have any real traffic problems, unlike previous races this size. I ran a solid consistent race throughout and knocked 72 seconds off last year's time for a new personal best of 44 minutes flat. Right foot and ankle is slightly sore today, but nothing major and I'm now looking forward to the Great South Run in Portsmouth in two weeks time.

This weekend also marks two weeks since the Berlin Marathon and ends my self imposed moratorium, so I can start thinking about new challenges... :-)

Wednesday, October 04, 2006

First post-marathon run - feels good

I went out for a run around Clapham Common yesterday. Slow easy pace, chatting all the while and just enjoying the evening. Then another runner came breezing past and I went after him, stretching the legs and enjoying a bit of pace. Feels really good, no aches, legs feel strong and happy.

Still hoping for a PB this weekend, although I haven't been doing the pace training, so might be running at the wrong pace for the first K or so. Will have to wait and see...
