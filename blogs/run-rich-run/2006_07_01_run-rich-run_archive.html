Wednesday, July 19, 2006

Mad dogs and Englishmen

Yesterday was mercilessly hot, around 32-34C with no clouds and only a light wind. Four of us went out regardless and two of us did the 7 mile canal loop. I carried 500mL of water, but still sufferred from the heat. Baseline heartrate was higher than usual, roughly 185 instead of 175. At about the 4 mile mark there is an incline up from the canal towpath to cross a road, and I saw a heartrate of 214 at that point, far higher than the previous highest I have seen (197). I backed right off at that point, not interested in risking heatstroke. Shortly afterwards I started pouring water on my head, which really helped to keep me cool. Gruelling run, and I'm still tired today, but no ill effects. Also recorded a reasonably good time for this route, even with my slowdown, which means I should be in good shape for the half-marathon distance. I should really start stretching out for some longer runs, but it's hard finding time since I am away most weekends. I am planning to fit in a long after work run in the week, but cancelled the first one next week almost as soon as I planned it...

Distance: 7 miles
Pace: medium
Terrain: flat on path
Time: approx 58:00
Average heartrate: 184
Max Heartrate: 214

Monday, July 17, 2006

Sprint training - UGH

Went for interval training run along the Lea Valley Navigation Canal at lunchtime today. After a short warm-up jog, ran sets of one minute sprint, followed by one minute jogging slowly. Ran five sets out, then turned and headed for home.

Died 40 seconds into sprint 7 and went back to slow jog and completed the last three sets at a "sprint" pace more like a medium jog, tail between my legs.

Still, this will be good training and hope to stick with it. I only had one other companion on this run who seemed happy with my pace. This should be a regular Monday session now.

Pace: interval
Terrain: flat on path
Time: approx 28:40
Average heartrate: 171 (?)

Thursday, July 13, 2006

Tower Bridge race

Ran the race route from Royal China (Canary Wharf) to Tower Bridge and back. This is a 5 mile route that a group from Credit Suisse run regularly. My previous best time for this route was 37:30, and I managed to beat this with a 37:19 today. Ran strong on the way out, faded a bit on the way back, but overall happy with this.

The running group that does this is now doing speedwork on Mondays, longer run (7-8 miles) on Tuesday and this 5 mile race route on Thursdays. If I can run with them I will be in good shape.

Distance: 5 miles
Pace: race
Terrain: flat on path
Time: 37'19"
Average heartrate: unknown
