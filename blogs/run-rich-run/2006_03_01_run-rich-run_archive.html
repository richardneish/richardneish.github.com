Sunday, March 05, 2006

18 miles!!

Well, it's been a while since the last update. I have been running, but haven't had the time to do any updates. Today I have something memorable, so I'll make the time.

Went for a little run with Charlotte this morning. Actually it pretty much took the whole day. I left home at 9:15am, started running about 10:45am after driving to Charlotte's place and then waiting for her to get ready. The route went on and on and on, going along the Thames from Rotherhide to Vauxhall Bridge on the south bank, then across the bridge and continuing west along the noth side of the river. At Chelsea Bridge we turned away from the river to the north. Ran through Sloane Square and up to Knightsbridge, then turned west along the south side of Hyde Park and Kensington Gardens, then continued along Kensington High Street and Kensington Churh Road. The corner of Kensington Church Road and Notting Hill Gate marked the most northwest corner of the route.

At this point we turned east along Bayswater Road and followed the north edge of Hyde Park along to Marble Arch. We turned right to head south on Park Lane to Nothumberland Gate, then down Constitution Hill to Buckingham Palace. Crossed in front of the palace, then down to Westminister, past the Houses of Parliment.

Then we turned east along the north side of the Thames, along the Victoria Embankment for a few miles. At Tower Bridge we crossed over to the south side and rejoined our outward route back east to Rotherhide. Total distance: 18.4 miles. Total time, 3 hours and 10 minutes.

This is the longest distance I have ever run, and although the pace was quite slow, I'm really pleased with it. I have sore knees now, but they don't feel injured, just tired. WIll see how I feel over the next few days...
