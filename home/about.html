UIUC / Champaign-Urbana

The University of Illinois has three main campuses, in Chicago, Springfield and Champaign-Urbana.  For historical reasons the Champaign-Urbana campus is called the University of Illinois at  Urbana-Champaign, while the twin city region is typically referred to as Champaign-Urbana (or Chambana, or Shampoo-Banana,...).  The university boasts the second largest university library system in the US, behind Harvard and the Department of Computer Science here has long been associated with computing excellence.  As far as regional attractions go, the surrounding cornfields provide little distraction from academic pursuits, a possible factor in the high standard of work produced here over the years.

I spend my days almost exclusively on the engineering campus, on the northern part of the university.  My lectures are held in the Digital Computer Laboratory or in Everitt Hall.  Outside of lectures I split my time between coursework, research and my part-time job as system administrator for the Image Formation and Processing Group and the Digital Signal Processing Group.  These groups have computer workstation labs in both the Computer and Systems Research Laboratory and the Beckman Institute for Advanced Science and Technology, where I have and office.  My research is in parallel programming techniques and my research advisor is Prof. Steven Lumetta.

Sailing

I am a rabid sailor, slowly drying out in the Midwestern plains.  Future plans involve making a trans-Atlantic passage within the next five years, and competing in an ocean race of 500+ miles.  Past sailing has included:
  • Pirates' Week Race:- Port Royal, Jamaica to Georgetown, Grand Cayman (3 times, most recently in 1995)
  • Passage from Paradise Island, Bahamas to Kingston, Jamaica on Kooyah (May 1997)
  • Passage from Plymouth, England to Ipswitch, England on Mermerus (Clipper 1) (August 1998)
  • Club racing on cruiser class yachts at the RJYC and MBYC(since 1986)
  • Dinghy racing at RJYC - 470 and JY15 classes (since 1994)
